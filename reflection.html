Reflect on Your Learning

Lesson 1

  1. Why is it necessary to find a common denominator when adding or subtracting fractions?
  2. What strategies can you use to find the least common denominator efficiently?
  3. How do fractions relate to real-life situations you encounter daily?

Self-Reflection Questions for Lesson 2

  1. How does multiplying fractions differ from adding fractions?
  2. Why is it important to simplify fractions after multiplying?
  3. In what real-life situations might you need to multiply fractions?

Consider writing down your answers in a journal or discussing them with a classmate.

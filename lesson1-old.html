Understanding Common Denominators

A common denominator is needed to add or subtract fractions. The least common denominator (LCD) is the smallest number that both denominators can divide into evenly.

Example:

To add \( \frac{1}{4} \) and \( \frac{1}{6} \), find the LCD:

  • Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16...
  • Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18...
  • LCD is 12.

Convert fractions:

  • \( \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{12} \)
  • \( \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{12} \)

Add the fractions:

\( \frac{3}{12} + \frac{2}{12} = \frac{5}{12} \)

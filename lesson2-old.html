Understanding How to Multiply Fractions

Multiplying fractions is straightforward:

  1. Multiply the numerators (top numbers).
  2. Multiply the denominators (bottom numbers).
  3. Simplify the resulting fraction if possible.

Example:

Multiply \( \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} \):

Numerators: \( 2 \times 4 = 8 \)

Denominators: \( 3 \times 5 = 15 \)

Result: \( \frac{8}{15} \)

Simplifying Fractions

If the resulting fraction can be simplified, divide the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor (GCF).

Multiplying Mixed Numbers

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before multiplying.

Example:

Multiply \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{2}{3} \):

Convert \( 1 \frac{1}{2} \) to improper fraction: \( \frac{3}{2} \)

Multiply: \( \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{2}{3} = \frac{6}{6} = 1 \)
